Pricing Psychology Tips for Solo Devs
The Sweet Spot Strategy

Good: 60% of clients choose this (your main revenue driver)
Better: 30% upgrade to this (higher margins)
Best: 10% go premium (prestige projects)

Positioning Language
Instead of: "I'm just a solo developer"
Say: "I specialize in fast, direct development without agency overhead"
Instead of: "It's cheaper because it's just me"
Say: "You get better value because there's no markup or middlemen"
Client Qualification Questions

"What's your budget range for this project?" (qualify early)
"When do you need this completed?" (rush pricing opportunity)
"Have you worked with developers before?" (set expectations)
"What happens if this project is successful?" (future work potential)

Payment Terms That Work

Small projects (<$5K): 50% up front, 50% on completion
Medium projects ($5-15K): 40% up front, 30% at midpoint, 30% completion
Large projects (>$15K): 25% up front, then monthly milestones

Red Flags to Avoid

"We don't have budget but will pay you in equity"
"This should be quick and easy" (usually isn't)
"We need this done yesterday"
"Can you match this $200 quote from overseas?"


Market Positioning
You're not competing with:

$5/hour overseas developers
Big agencies charging $200/hour
DIY website builders

You're competing with:

Other skilled solo/freelance developers
Small development shops (2-5 people)
Agencies for smaller projects

Your ideal clients:

Small businesses with $50K+ revenue
Startups with some funding
Agencies outsourcing technical work
Established businesses needing custom solutions
